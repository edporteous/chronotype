About Chronotype

Chronotype is an attribute of human beings, reflecting at what time of the day their physical functions (hormone level, body temperature, cognitive faculties, eating and sleeping) are active, change or reach a certain level. This phenomenon is commonly reduced to sleeping habits only, referring to people as "larks" and "owls" which refer, respectively, to morning people (those who wake up early and are most alert in the first part of the day) and evening people (those who are most alert in the late evening hours and prefer to go to bed late).

About The Other Way Works

The Other Way Works creates daring and remarkable theatre that draws the audience into the very heart of the experience.

"The Other Way Works is a dynamic young company that is successfully exploring the possibilities of what theatre can and might be."
Lyn Gardner, The Guardian

The Other Way Works is a Birmingham based theatre company formed in 2001. They create highly interactive performance experiences, frequently for small groups of audiences. Their focus is on your experience as an audience member, and they see the performance of the work as the offering of a gift to each member of the audience.

They create their work in a deeply collaborative style, and constantly push these collaborations in new ways.

Their work is usually site-specific or responsive, and they place great importance on ensuring that the narrative and dramaturgy of the work reflects the site in which it is performed, and that the form compliments the content.

The Company is made up of a group of talented artists brought together under the artistic direction of Katie Day.
